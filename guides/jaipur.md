# Jaipur — Curated Travel Guide

## Overview
Jaipur, the "Pink City," is the capital of Rajasthan and a gateway to
India's royal heritage. Its old city is enclosed by pink-washed sandstone
walls and laid out on a precise grid, a rarity for a city planned in the
18th century.

## Off-beat spots (beyond Hawa Mahal and Amber Fort)
- **Panna Meena ka Kund**: A stunning symmetrical stepwell tucked into a
  residential lane near Amber Fort — far quieter than the fort itself and
  a favorite for photographers at sunrise.
- **Nahargarh Biological Park**: A large, walkable wildlife park on the
  outskirts, good for a slower half-day away from the crowded forts.
- **Anokhi Museum of Hand Printing**: A small museum inside a restored
  haveli in Amber, dedicated to traditional block-printing techniques —
  rarely crowded and includes live demonstrations.
- **Bagru village**: About 30km from the city center, this village is a
  center for natural-dye block printing; visitors can watch artisans work
  and buy directly from print workshops.

## Local culture and etiquette
- Bargaining is expected in most markets (Johari Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar) but
  is not customary in fixed-price government emporiums.
- Modest dress is appreciated when visiting temples; shoulders and knees
  covered is a safe default.
- Rajasthani thali meals are typically eaten with the right hand; cutlery
  is offered on request at most restaurants catering to tourists.

## Best time to visit
October to March is the most comfortable window — daytime temperatures
are mild and evenings are cool. Summers (April–June) are extremely hot,
often exceeding 40°C.

## Local transport tips
Auto-rickshaws are the most common short-distance transport within the
city; agreeing on a fare beforehand (or insisting on the meter) avoids
overcharging. Ride-hailing apps operate reliably in Jaipur and are
generally a safer bet for visitors unfamiliar with local fare norms.
