# Manali — Curated Travel Guide

## Overview
Manali sits in the Kullu Valley in Himachal Pradesh and serves as the
main gateway to high-altitude passes like Rohtang and Atal Tunnel. Old
Manali and the main town have distinctly different characters — the
former quieter and more backpacker-oriented, the latter busier and more
commercial.

## Off-beat spots (beyond Solang Valley and Hadimba Temple)
- **Jibhi**: A small village roughly 3 hours from Manali, largely
  untouched by mass tourism, known for wooden architecture, pine forests,
  and slower-paced homestays.
- **Naggar Castle**: A restored 15th-century castle-turned-heritage-hotel
  overlooking the Kullu valley, with far fewer visitors than Manali's
  main attractions.
- **Bijli Mahadev**: A temple reachable via a steep trek near Kullu, with
  panoramic valley views and a much smaller crowd than typical roadside
  temples.
- **Sethan village**: A remote hamlet above Old Manali, popular in winter
  for quieter snow experiences away from Solang Valley's commercial
  crowds.

## Local culture and etiquette
- Local Himachali cuisine (siddu, dham) is distinct from typical North
  Indian restaurant fare and worth seeking out in smaller local eateries
  rather than tourist-focused restaurants.
- Modest dress is expected at temples; some, including Hadimba Temple,
  have specific customs around footwear and photography.
- Bargaining is common for taxis and adventure activity operators but
  less so in fixed-menu restaurants.

## Best time to visit
March to June suits pleasant weather and valley views; December to
February suits snow activities, though some higher roads (like Rohtang
Pass) close due to snowfall and require permits or seasonal timing.

## Local transport tips
Shared taxis are common for reaching outlying villages not served by
regular buses; negotiating a fare before departure is standard practice.
Renting a scooter or bike is popular for exploring the valley at one's
own pace, though mountain roads demand caution, especially near passes.
