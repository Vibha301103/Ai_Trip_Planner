# Goa — Curated Travel Guide

## Overview
Goa's identity splits cleanly between North Goa (livelier, more
nightlife-driven) and South Goa (quieter, more upscale and residential).
Its Portuguese colonial history still shows up in architecture, food, and
local surnames.

## Off-beat spots (beyond Baga and Calangute beaches)
- **Cola Beach**: A secluded cove south of Agonda, backed by a freshwater
  lagoon — reachable mainly by a short trek or scooter, with far fewer
  visitors than the main beaches.
- **Divar Island**: A short ferry ride from Old Goa, this island has
  almost no tourist infrastructure — mostly paddy fields, old Portuguese
  churches, and quiet village lanes, best explored by bicycle.
- **Chorla Ghat**: A hill-forest region on the Goa-Karnataka border with
  waterfalls and birdwatching spots, popular with locals but rarely
  visited by tourists on standard itineraries.
- **Fontainhas (Latin Quarter, Panaji)**: A preserved old quarter of
  narrow lanes and pastel Portuguese-era houses — best explored on foot
  early morning before the heat sets in.

## Local culture and etiquette
- Beachwear is fine on the beach itself, but modest clothing is expected
  when entering churches, temples, or local village areas away from the
  coast.
- Many beach shacks close during the monsoon season (June–September) as
  the sea becomes rough and unsafe for swimming.
- Alcohol is comparatively cheap and widely available due to lower state
  excise duties, but public intoxication is frowned upon outside the
  main tourist beach belts.

## Best time to visit
November to February offers the most pleasant weather — warm days, cool
evenings, calm sea. The monsoon (June–September) turns Goa lush and green
but rules out most beach and water activities.

## Local transport tips
Scooters and motorbikes are the dominant way locals and tourists get
around; an international driving permit or valid license is technically
required and occasionally checked. Prepaid taxi counters exist at the
airport and major bus stands for a fixed, non-negotiable fare.
